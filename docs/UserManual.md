# Maria's Notebook — User Guide for Teachers

**A complete guide to managing your Montessori classroom**

---

# Getting Started

## First Launch

When you open Maria's Notebook for the first time, you'll see a four-page welcome walkthrough:

1. **Welcome** — An overview of what the app does: manage students, lessons, work, and observations all in one place.
2. **Add Your Students** — A reminder to start by adding your students. You can organize them by level and track their lesson history.
3. **Plan Your Lessons** — An introduction to the lesson library and the work lifecycle (active, review, complete).
4. **Quick Notes & More** — How to use the floating button for fast access to notes, presentations, work items, and to-dos.

Tap **Next** to move through each page, or tap **Skip** to jump straight into the app. On the last page, tap **Get Started** to begin.

## The Main Screen

After setup, you'll land on the **Today** view — your daily dashboard. Along the top of the screen you'll see navigation pills for the main areas of the app:

| Tab | What it does |
|-----|-------------|
| Today | Your daily hub — lessons, work, reminders, attendance |
| Attendance | Mark students present, tardy, or absent |
| Note | Quick observation entry |
| Students | Your student roster and profiles |
| Supplies | Classroom inventory |
| Procedures | Documented classroom routines |
| Meetings | Student meeting scheduler |
| Lessons | Your full lesson library |
| More | Access to planning tools, projects, AI chat, settings, and everything else |

## The Floating Button

In the bottom-right corner of every screen, you'll see a small glass-style button. This is your **quick action hub** — the fastest way to do almost anything in the app.

| Gesture | What happens |
|---------|-------------|
| Double-tap | Opens the Command Bar (type a command in plain English) |
| Long-press (hold for about half a second) | Opens the Pie Menu with five quick actions |
| Drag | Moves the button to a new position on screen (it stays where you put it) |

### The Pie Menu

When you long-press the floating button, five colored segments fan out in a circle:

| Segment | Color | What it does |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Present | Blue | Record or schedule a lesson presentation |
| Work | Orange | Create a new work item |
| Practice | Pink | Log a practice session |
| Todo | Green | Add a to-do for yourself |
| Note | Purple | Write a quick observation |

Slide your finger to the action you want and release.

### The Command Bar

Double-tap the floating button to open a text field where you can type commands in plain English:

- "Gave Sophia long division" — records a presentation
- "Note about Emma's counting — struggling with teen numbers" — creates an observation note
- "Todo: buy more bead bars by Friday" — creates a to-do with a due date
- "Assign counting work to Leo" — creates a work item

The app parses your words, identifies students and lessons by name, and fills in the details. You'll see a confirmation before anything is saved.

# Today View

The Today view is your daily command center. Everything you need for the current day is here.

## Navigating Dates

At the top, you'll see today's date with arrow buttons on either side. Tap the arrows to move forward or back one school day (weekends and holidays you've marked as non-school days are automatically skipped).

## Sections

The Today view is organized into collapsible sections. From top to bottom:

### Attendance Strip

A quick row of student indicators showing who's present, absent, or not yet marked. Tap to expand the full attendance grid.

### Reminders

Any reminders due today or overdue from previous days.

### Lessons Due

Presentations you've scheduled for today. Tap a lesson to open it, view the lesson plan, and mark it as given.

### Meetings

Student meetings scheduled for today. Tap to open the meeting and record notes.

### Scheduled Check-Ins

Work items with check-ins due today. Tap to log your observation.

### Todos

Your to-do items for today. Tap the checkbox to mark them complete.

### Completed Items

A collapsed section at the bottom showing everything you've finished today — completed work, given lessons, and finished meetings. Useful for reviewing your day.

# Students

## Viewing Your Roster

Tap **Students** in the navigation to see your full student list. Each student card shows their name, level (Lower or Upper Elementary), and a summary of their recent activity.

You can sort the list by:

- Alphabetical order
- Last lesson given
- Age or grade level
- Upcoming birthdays

Use the search bar at the top to find a student quickly.

## Adding a Student

Tap the **Add Student** button (or use the add icon). Fill in:

- **First Name** (required)
- **Last Name** (required)
- **Nickname** (optional — used for display if set)
- **Birthday** (date picker)
- **Start Date** (when the student joined your class)
- **Level** — Lower Elementary or Upper Elementary

Tap **Add** and the student appears in your roster immediately.

## Student Detail View

Tap any student to open their full profile. You'll see several tabs:

### Progress Tab

An overview of where this student stands across the curriculum. Shows lesson completion by subject and group, with visual indicators for active work, items in review, and mastered lessons.

### Meetings Tab

All one-on-one meetings with this student. You can schedule new meetings, view past meeting notes, and track outcomes.

### Notes Tab

A timeline of every observation you've recorded for this student. Filter by category (Academic, Behavioral, Social, Emotional, Health, General) or search by text. Newest notes appear first.

### Files Tab

Documents attached to this student's profile — report cards, assessments, photos of work samples. Tap to view or add new files.

# Lessons

## The Lesson Library

Tap **Lessons** to browse your curriculum. Lessons are organized hierarchically:

- **Subjects** (e.g., Math, Language, Science, Practical Life, Sensorial)
  - **Groups** within each subject (e.g., "Counting," "Phonics," "Botany")
    - **Individual lessons** in each group

You can search by lesson name, filter by subject, or scroll through the full library.

## Lesson Detail

Tap any lesson to see its full details:

| Section | What it shows |
|---------|-------------|
| Name & Subject | Title, subject, and group |
| Write-Up | The full lesson plan and presentation script |
| Materials | What you need to have ready |
| Purpose | The educational objective |
| Age Range | Recommended student ages |
| Teacher Notes | Your personal notes about teaching this lesson |
| Follow-Up Work | Suggested activities for after the presentation |
| Attachments | PDF files, images, or Pages documents |
| Lesson Journey | A timeline showing which students have received this lesson and their progress |

## Adding a Lesson

Tap the add button from the Lessons screen. Fill in:

- **Name** (required)
- **Subject** (required — choose from existing subjects or create a new one)
- **Group** (required — choose from existing groups or create a new one)
- **Format** — Standard (a regular lesson) or Story (a lesson with parent-child branching for narrative-based curriculum)
- **Source** — Album (from your Montessori album), Personal (your own lesson), or Great Lesson
- **Materials, Purpose, Age Range, Teacher Notes, Suggested Follow-Up Work** (all optional but recommended)

You can also **import lessons from a CSV file** for bulk entry — useful when setting up your library for the first time.

## Story-Format Lessons

If you choose the Story format, you can create branching lessons: a parent story with child chapters that extend it. This is useful for cosmic education and narrative-based Montessori curriculum where one Great Lesson branches into many follow-up explorations.

# Presentations

A **presentation** is the act of giving a lesson to one or more students. This is how you track what you've taught and to whom.

## Recording a Presentation

There are several ways to record a presentation:

1. **From the Pie Menu** — Long-press the floating button, select the blue "Present" segment
2. **From a Lesson** — Open a lesson's detail view and tap "Give Lesson" or "Assign"
3. **From the Today View** — Tap a scheduled lesson to open and record it
4. **From the Command Bar** — Type something like "Gave Emma long division"

## The Presentation Form

When you create a presentation, you'll fill in:

### Lesson

Choose which lesson you're presenting. You can search by name or browse by subject.

### Students

Select one or more students who will receive this lesson. You can search by name and select multiple students.

### Date

By default, the date is set to today. You can:

- **Mark as Given** — Record it as already presented (today or a past date)
- **Schedule for Later** — Set a future date when you plan to give the lesson

### Notes

Free-form text for recording how the presentation went — what the student responded to, what was difficult, any observations.

### Follow-Up Options

After recording a presentation, you can set flags:

- **Needs Practice** — The student should practice this independently
- **Needs Another Presentation** — You need to re-present this lesson
- **Follow-Up Work** — Describe what follow-up activity the student should do

These flags feed into the Inbox, so you'll be reminded to follow up.

## Presentation States

Each presentation moves through a lifecycle:

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Draft | Created but not yet scheduled or given |
| Scheduled | Assigned to a specific future date |
| Presented | Lesson has been given — this becomes a permanent historical record |

Once a presentation is marked as "Presented," the lesson title and details are frozen as a snapshot. Even if you later edit the lesson, the historical record of what you presented remains unchanged.

# Work

**Work** represents the independent practice and follow-up activities students do after receiving a lesson. Work items track a student's progress from initial assignment through mastery.

## How Work Gets Created

Work items are typically created:

- **After a presentation** — When you record giving a lesson and check "Needs Practice" or describe follow-up work
- **Manually** — From the Pie Menu (orange "Work" segment) or the Command Bar
- **From the Inbox** — When follow-up recommendations appear and you act on them

## Work Detail

Each work item shows:

- **Title** — The name of the work (often matching the lesson name)
- **Student(s)** — Who is assigned
- **Lesson** — The lesson this work relates to
- **Status** — Active, Review, or Complete
- **Due Date** — When you expect the work to be finished (optional)
- **Steps** — A breakdown of the work into individual steps (optional)
- **Check-Ins** — Your observations recorded during the work period
- **Notes** — Any observations attached to this work item

## The Work Lifecycle

```
Active → Review → Complete
```

| Status | What it means |
|--------|-------------|
| Active | The student is currently working on this |
| Review | The student may be ready — you need to assess |
| Complete | The student has finished — record the outcome |

When you mark work as complete, you can record a **completion outcome**: mastered, needs review, or needs more practice. This informs the curriculum progression tracking.

## Check-Ins

A check-in is a quick observation you record while a student is working. To log one:

1. Open the work item
2. Tap "Add Check-In"
3. Write your observation (what you saw, how the student is progressing)
4. Optionally rate the quality
5. Optionally schedule the next check-in

Check-ins build a timeline of progress for each work item.

## Practice Sessions

A **practice session** is a quick log of students practicing during work time. You can record:

- Which students were practicing
- Which work item(s) they were on
- Duration
- Whether it was individual or group practice
- Quality observations

Access this from the Pie Menu (pink "Practice" segment).

# Notes & Observations

Notes are the primary way to capture classroom observations. You can attach them to specific students, link them to lessons, and categorize them for later reference.

## Creating a Note

### From the Pie Menu

Long-press the floating button and select the purple "Note" segment. A quick note sheet opens with:

- **Student(s)** — Select one or more students (or "All Students" for a general classroom observation)
- **Body** — Your observation text
- **Tags** — Optional labels for categorization
- **Date** — Defaults to now, but you can change it
- **Photo** — Attach a picture (optional)

### From the Command Bar

Type something like:

- "Note about Emma — great focus during counting today"
- "Observation: Leo helped a younger student with practical life"

### From a Student's Profile

Open the student's detail view, go to the Notes tab, and tap the add button.

## Note Scope

Each note can be scoped to:

- **All students** — A general classroom observation
- **One student** — Specific to that child
- **Multiple students** — An observation about a group interaction

## Tags and Categories

Notes can be tagged with custom labels. Common categories include Academic, Behavioral, Social, Emotional, Health, and General. You can create your own tags in Settings.

## Viewing Notes

Notes appear in several places:

- **Student detail view** — Notes tab shows all observations for that student
- **Today view** — Recent notes from today
- **Notes section** (from the navigation) — Search and filter across all notes

## Note Templates

In Settings, you can create templates — pre-written observation starters that save time during busy classroom moments. Templates can include default tags and category settings.

# Todos

Todos are your personal task list — things you need to do that aren't directly student work items.

## Creating a Todo

From the Pie Menu (green "Todo" segment) or the Command Bar:

- **Title** (required) — What you need to do
- **Description** (optional) — Additional details
- **Due Date** (optional) — When it's due
- **Recurrence** — None, Daily, Weekdays, Weekly, Biweekly, Monthly, Yearly, or Custom
- **Subtasks** — Break the todo into smaller steps

### Smart Parsing

The Command Bar understands natural language for todos:

- "Buy bead bars by Friday" — Creates a todo with title "Buy bead bars" and due date set to Friday
- "Finish report daily" — Creates a recurring daily todo
- "Remind me to call Emma's parents" — Creates a todo titled "Call Emma's parents"

## Todos in Today View

Your incomplete todos for today appear in a dedicated section. Tap the checkbox to mark them complete. Recurring todos automatically generate the next occurrence.

## Subtasks

Complex todos can have subtasks — a checklist within the todo. Check off each subtask as you complete it. The parent todo tracks how many subtasks are done.

# Supplies

Track your classroom materials and inventory so you always know what you have and what needs restocking.

## Supplies List

Tap **Supplies** in the navigation to see all your tracked materials. Each supply shows:

- Name
- Status badge — **In Stock** (green), **Low** (yellow), **Critical** (orange), or **Out of Stock** (red)
- Category and location

## Adding a Supply

Tap the add button and fill in:

- **Name** (required) — e.g., "Colored Pencils"
- **Category** (required) — Materials, Books, Equipment, Consumables, Art Supplies, Food, or Other
- **Current Quantity** — How many you have now
- **Minimum Threshold** — The quantity at which the status switches to "Low"
- **Reorder Amount** — How many to order when restocking
- **Location** — Where it's stored in your classroom
- **Notes** — Any additional information

## Managing Inventory

From a supply's detail view, you can:

- **Adjust quantity** — Add or remove stock
- **Mark as on order** — Flag that you've ordered more
- **View transaction history** — See all additions and removals over time

The status automatically updates based on your current quantity versus the minimum threshold.

# Planning Tools

Tap **More** in the navigation to access the full set of planning tools.

## Open Work

A view of all active work items across all students. Filter by student, subject, or status. Use this to plan which students need check-ins and what work needs attention.

## Checklist

A class-wide checklist view organized by subject and group. See at a glance which students have received which lessons. Check off items as you go — useful for weekly or monthly planning.

A pie chart showing how your lesson time is distributed across subjects. Helps you ensure a balanced Montessori curriculum and identify areas that may need more attention.

A chart showing how often each lesson has been presented. Identify under-taught lessons that need more focus or over-taught ones where you can move on.

# Inbox

The Inbox is your follow-up assistant. It automatically collects items that need your attention and organizes them by urgency.

## How Items Get Into the Inbox

- Presentations marked "Needs Practice" or "Needs Another Presentation"
- Work items that haven't had a check-in recently
- Work items in "Review" status that haven't been completed
- Overdue scheduled items

## Inbox Buckets

| Bucket | What it means |
|--------|-------------|
| Overdue | Items that have gone too long without attention (lessons >7 days, work >5 days, reviews >3 days) |
| Due Today | Items scheduled for today |
| Inbox | Items needing action but not yet overdue |
| Upcoming | Items scheduled for the future |

## Acting on Inbox Items

Tap any item to open it. From there you can:

- Schedule a check-in
- Mark work as complete
- Re-present a lesson
- Reschedule for later
- Dismiss if no longer relevant

# Progression & Tracks

## Tracks

A **track** is a defined curriculum path — a sequence of lessons and skills a student moves through over time. Tracks help you ensure students progress systematically.

### Setting Up Tracks

In Settings or from the Progression area, you can create tracks with:

- Track name (e.g., "Math: Counting to Multiplication")
- Steps — The ordered sequence of lessons or milestones
- Which students are enrolled

### Monitoring Progress

The Progression view shows each student's position in their enrolled tracks. You can see who's ahead, who's on pace, and who may need additional support.

## Group Tracks

Group tracks let you apply the same progression template to multiple students at once, while still tracking individual progress.

# AI Assistant

Maria's Notebook includes an AI chat feature powered by Claude. It can analyze your classroom data and answer questions.

## Setup

Before using AI features, you need to add your Anthropic API key:

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Find the **AI / Chat** section
3. Enter your API key
4. Choose your preferred model (Claude 3 Opus for best quality, Sonnet for balanced, Haiku for fastest)

## What You Can Ask

The AI has access to your classroom data and can answer questions like:

- "Which students haven't received a lesson in the past two weeks?"
- "Summarize Emma's progress this month"
- "What's my curriculum balance across subjects?"
- "Which work items are overdue?"
- "Help me plan next week's lessons"
- "Generate a progress summary for parent conferences"

## How to Access

Tap **Ask AI** from the More menu. You'll see a chat interface where you can type questions and receive responses. The AI reads your actual student, lesson, and work data to give relevant answers.

# Backup & Restore

Protect your data by creating regular backups.

## Creating a Backup

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Tap **Backup & Restore**
3. Tap **Create Backup**
4. Choose where to save the file (Files app, iCloud Drive, or local storage)
5. Optionally set a password to encrypt the backup
6. A confirmation toast appears when the backup is complete

The backup file is a JSON document containing all your students, lessons, presentations, work items, notes, and settings.

## Restoring from a Backup

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Tap **Backup & Restore**
3. Tap **Restore from Backup**
4. Select your backup file
5. Choose restore mode:
   - **Merge** — Adds missing data from the backup while keeping your current data
   - **Replace** — Replaces all current data with the backup contents
6. Wait for the restore to complete (a progress bar shows status)

## iCloud Sync

If you enable CloudKit in Settings, your data automatically syncs across your devices via iCloud. This is separate from manual backups — it happens continuously in the background.

# Tips for Daily Use

## Start Your Day with Today View

Open the app each morning and check the Today view. It shows everything on your plate — scheduled lessons, pending work check-ins, meetings, reminders, and todos. Use the date arrows to look ahead at tomorrow or review yesterday.

## Use the Floating Button Constantly

The Pie Menu and Command Bar are designed for the pace of a real classroom. When you notice something:

- **Long-press** and slide to "Note" to capture an observation in seconds
- **Long-press** and slide to "Practice" to log what students are working on
- **Double-tap** and type a quick command like "Gave Leo botany"

## Let the Inbox Guide Your Follow-Ups

Don't try to remember every follow-up. When you record a presentation with "Needs Practice" or describe follow-up work, the Inbox automatically tracks it and reminds you when it's time to check in.

## Use Check-Ins to Build a Progress Story

Every time you observe a student working, log a quick check-in on their work item. Over time, these build a detailed picture of each student's journey — invaluable for parent conferences and progress reports.

## Review Progression Weekly

Set aside time each week (perhaps Friday afternoon) to open the Progression view and see where each student stands. This helps you plan the next week's presentations and ensures no student falls behind.

## Back Up Regularly

Create a manual backup at least once a week, or enable auto-backup in Settings. Your classroom data is irreplaceable — protect it.
